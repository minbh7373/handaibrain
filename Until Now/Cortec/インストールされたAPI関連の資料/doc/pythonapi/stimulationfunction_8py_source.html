1 '''@doctring stimulationfunction
2 
3  A stimulation function defines what stimulation signal is applied
4  to which electrodes.
5 
6  A stimulation function consists of
7  - a sequence of stimulation atoms that can be repeated a number of
8  times
9  - and the information which electrodes to use for stimulation.
10 
11  There are two types of stimulation that consist of the following atoms:
12 
13  1) A stimulation pause: one pause atom
14  2) A stimulation pulse: five 4-rect stimulation atoms
15 
16  The stimulation atoms in the second case each describe one specific part
17  of the stimulation pulse, including main and counter pulses as well
18  as dead zones between them. The five stimulation atoms form a stimulation
19  cove of the form:
20  ____
21  Pulse _ _| |_ _____
22  | |
23  |_|
24 
25  Atom 1 2 3 4 5
26 
27  Pulse Atom Definition:
28 
29  1) Main pulse: Holds the amplitude and duration of the main pulse
30  in uA and us. The acceptable value range goes from
31  -6120 to 0 uA.
32  The granularity changes for smaller amplitudes as follows:
33  amplitude >= -3060 uA: step size of 12
34  amplitude < -3060 uA: step size of 24
35  This leads to a set of acceptable values that looks like:
36  [-6120, -6096, ..., -3084, -3060, -3048, ..., -12, 0 ]
37 
38  Values for the pulse duration can be set in steps of 10 us.
39  The acceptable range is between 10 and 2550 us.
40  2) Dead zone 0: Holds the duration of the pause between main and
41  counter pulse in us. Must have an amplitude of 0.
42  Values can be set in steps of 10 us. The acceptable
43  range is between 10 and 2550 us.
44  3) Counter pulse: Holds the amplitude and duration of the
45  counter pulse in uA and us.
46  The counter amplitude strength must be
47  -1/4 * main_pulse_amplitude.
48  The counter amplitude duration must be
49  4 * main_pulse_duration.
50  4) Dead zone 0: Must be identical to the atom 2)
51  5) Dead zone 1: Holds the duration of the pause after the pulse was
52  delivered. Must have an amplitude of 0. Values can be
53  set in steps of 80 us. The acceptable range goes from
54  10 to 20400 us.
55  Note that the steps are starting from 0 while the minimal
56  value is 10 us. This leads to a set of acceptable values
57  that looks like: [10, 80, 160, 240, ... , 20400]
58 
59  Stimulation is usually applied between two points: one source
60  electrode and one destination electrode (e.g., a ground electrode).
61  Here, this concept is generalized to so-called 'virtual
62  electrodes'. A virtual electrode is a non-empty set of electrodes.
63  It allows more degrees of freedom to shape the electric field of a
64  stimulation. These two sets have to be disjunct (because an
65  electrode is either a source or a destination electrode, not both)
66  and non-empty (because at least two electrodes are needed for
67  stimulation).
68 
69  Each electrode is addressed with a positive integer index (i.e.
70  uint32_t) and virtual electrodes are defined as sets of uint32_t.
71  Depending on the actual implant used, these indices may vary.
72  In addition, depending on the actually used implant, the electrode
73  indices and the allowed combinations of electrodes into virtual
74  electrodes may differ. Please refer to the documentation of the
75  used implant for more details on this.
76 
77  Typical usage:
78  1. create stimulationfunction instance with an
79  stimulationcommandfactory.
80  2. Add an atom to the stimulation function.
81  3. Repeat 2. until all atoms are added.
82  4. Set repetitions using setRepetitions().
83 
84  Please note that one function may only contain atoms of the same
85  type (e.g., only rectangular atoms).
86 
87  By adding the constant SRC_CHANNEL_GROUND_ELECTRODE to the destination
88  electrodes, stimulation to ground is enabled.
89 '''
